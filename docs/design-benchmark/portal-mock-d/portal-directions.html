Why C — the research capsule

Standing always visible kills forgot-to-renew churn (roughly 32% of lapses trace to the member simply never seeing where they stood).

One weighted next action beats a menu of equals — a member acts on the single real to-do, not a wall of options pretending to be equally urgent.

The equal-weight tile wall is the named anti-pattern: three same-size cards read as three same-priority asks, which is rarely true.

An empty state is a teachable moment, not a blank — both mocks’ all-clear states spend that moment on warmth or on what’s next.

Off-season mode is reassure-and-anticipate: no manufactured urgency, one line pointing at what’s coming.

Verdict: A · B · C · D · hybrid tweaks. A leads with standing — a full-bleed band makes “you’re current” the first thing read, at the cost of a heavier top-of-page and a two-column working area that trends toward an equal-weight tile wall and degrades on mobile-majority traffic. B reads as one more calm club page, and its all-clear state does real emotional work by sitting inline as the page’s own opening beat. C carries A’s band-first standing signature into B’s single calm column, keeps exactly one weighted action instead of a tile menu, and gives the dormant months their own reassure-and-anticipate job. None of the three spends fireweed; C reserves it for a future renewal-season CTA. Open question worth Geoff’s eye: does the value-mirror line in C earn its place, or does the facts-style “Your membership” block from B belong in C too.

D is the ruled direction (C’s function, A’s body) — confirm, tweak, or push further. D keeps A’s full-bleed band and two-column working area — now breathing at the desktop’s own width instead of the site’s prose measure — but rules the rail into subordinate reference material: one type step down, muted eyebrow labels, a fainter hairline border, a real 22rem column, and stacked two-line asset rows that fix A’s cramped mid-phrase wrap. The one weighted action and the value-mirror line both carry over from C unchanged, and the off-season variant keeps C’s reassure-and-anticipate close with the rail left as-is (reference is always valid). Mobile is licensed to be its own composition rather than a collapse of the desktop grid: a compact masthead, the one action, then flat full-width reference rows in recognition order, with the Classes tile dropped from that order (still reachable via the doors row) since an occasional phone check doesn’t need its empty-state line. Fireweed stays unspent in D too, reserved for the same future renewal-season CTA.
