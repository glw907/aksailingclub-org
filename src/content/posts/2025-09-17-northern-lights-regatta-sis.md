Ahoy sailors! The SIs for the Northern Lights Regatta are below. And note that we moved the start time up by an hour, so the skippers meeting is at 3:00 p.m. See ya there!

### Northern Lights Regatta Sailing Instructions

**September 20th, 2025**
**Alaska Sailing Club, Big Lake, Alaska**

**WELCOME:** The Northern Lights Regatta is our final racing event of the season. It’s a one-day-only series held on the Saturday before the fall work party and is decidedly low-key. We started this race as a homage to the highly informal “old-school” racing of the ASC’s past, where we start by the dock and sail around various islands in the lake.

This event has gained popularity in recent years due to the reliable September winds and the exciting challenge of using the landforms on the lake to the best possible advantage. Also, the shorter days improve the odds of viewing the Northern Lights while hanging by the campfire after the races!

ASC regattas are fun and welcoming for all sailors — new or experienced. Don’t have a boat or crew? Come anyway, and we’ll help you get on the water. Even if you do not want to race, you are welcome to sail recreationally, enjoy the lake, and join us for the potluck or other social events.

**RULES:** Racing will be governed by [The Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS)](https://d7qh6ksdplczd.cloudfront.net/sailing/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/29083752/2025-2028-RRS-with-Changes-and-Corrections.pdf) and these Sailing Instructions. The focus is on friendly competition and supporting new sailors.

**ENTRY AND FEES:** There’s no advance registration or entry fee for the Northern Lights Regatta.

**ELIGIBILITY:** Any member of the Alaska Sailing Club or their guest may participate. Each boat must have at least one ASC member on board. Club boats are available on a first-rigged/first-served basis, and once a skipper claims a club boat, it is theirs for the duration of the regatta.

**ADDITIONAL INFORMATION**: Keelboat sailors are encouraged to attend this one. 

#### Schedule and Check-In

**SCHEDULE:**
**Saturday, September 20** – The skipper’s meeting will be held at 3:00 PM. All boats must be fully rigged and ready to sail before the skipper’s meeting begins. Check-in happens at the skipper’s meeting. Experienced members are available to assist new sailors with rigging. But if you’d like help, please start working on your boat at least 90 minutes before the skipper’s meeting.

The first warning signal will be no earlier than 30 minutes after the skipper’s meeting, which allows time to launch and sail to the starting area. The race committee may delay the start if wind conditions are unsuitable for racing.

All races will be held on this day. The Northern Lights Regatta will have a target of 3 races, but the actual number will depend on wind and weather conditions.

**WARMUP RECOMMENDATION:** We strongly recommend arriving early enough for some warm-up sailing before the races. Taking some space to practice (tacks, jibes, spinnaker hoists and douses, upwind sailing, etc.) without the pressure of a race will help you be more relaxed and effective during the regatta. If possible, arriving the evening before is even better, as winds at that time are often the most consistent.

***Evening sailing****:* Winds on Big Lake are often light and variable in the afternoon, with stronger and more consistent “evening fill” winds usually arriving after 5:00 p.m. Because of this, race starts may be delayed, and sailors should be prepared to sail later in the day.

#### Other Activities

**SOCIAL EVENTS:** We’ll have a potluck will follow the races. The theme is *warm food* to cut the September chill. The potluck will take place once the day’s racing is complete.

**PHOTO SHARING:** We always encourage sailors to post event photos and stories in the `#general` or `#racing` channels on the ASC Discord server to celebrate the regatta and share the fun with fellow sailors.

#### Fleets

**FLEETS:** Per Northern Lights Regatta tradition, we’ll race in a single Portsmouth Fleet.

**RATING NOTE FOR BUCCANEERS**: Buccaneers must register as either spinnaker (rating 87) or non-spinnaker (rating 87.87). Each boat must declare its configuration at the skipper’s meeting and use it for the entire regatta.

#### Race Area and Courses

**RACING AREA**: Big Lake in the vicinity of ASC.

**COURSES:** The race committee will set a single mark to serve as one end of the starting line and will use the end of the “B” dock as the other end. Instead of using marks, they set a course using various islands on Big Lake. The race committee may designate different “course islands” for different fleets, and they will provide course information during the skippers meeting.

**MARKS**: A white mark will be used for the pin (non-boat) end of the start/finish line. All other course “marks” will be islands in Big Lake.

#### Starts and Finishes

**START AND FINISH LINES**: The start/finish line is between a white mark and the end of B dock. Starts and finishes use this same line. To finish, cross the line from the course side.

**STARTING SEQUENCE** Each race’s start will use the standard 5-4-1-0 minute sequence described in Rule 26 of the *Racing Rules of Sailing*. Often, the race committee will sound a few short courtesy horn blasts about six minutes before the start to get everyone’s attention and signal that the sequence is about to begin.

At five minutes before the start, the class flag for your fleet will be raised. Four minutes before the start, the preparatory flag will be raised. At one minute before the start, the preparatory flag will be lowered. Finally, at the starting signal (zero), the class flag will be lowered, and the race begins.

Watch the flags and listen for the horns — together they give you a clear countdown to the start. If it isn’t your fleet’s turn to start, please stay well away from the starting line area. This means don’t sail across or hover near the line while another fleet is in their starting sequence. Keeping the line clear helps the other sailors start fairly and makes things less confusing for everyone.

**FLAGS USED DURING THE STARTS:**

- **Class flag for the Buccaneer 18 fleet**: White with two black crossed sabers. Hoisted 5 minutes before the start, lowered at the start.

- **Class flag for the Portsmouth fleet**: White with two black crossed sabers. Hoisted 5 minutes before the start, lowered at the start.

- **Preparatory flag (P flag)**: Blue with a white square in the center. Hoisted 4 minutes before the start, lowered 1 minute before the start.

**OTHER COMMON FLAGS**:

- **Postponement (AP)**: Red/white vertical stripes. Hoisted when racing is postponed, lowered with one sound one minute before the new warning signal.

- **General recall (First Substitute)**: Yellow triangle on blue. Hoisted when all boats in a start must restart, lowered with one sound at the new warning signal.

- **Individual recall (X flag)**: White with blue cross. Hoisted when one or more boats are over early, lowered when those boats have returned or after 4 minutes, whichever is sooner.

- **Lifejackets required (Y flag)**: Red and yellow diagonal stripes. Hoisted when all sailors must wear lifejackets while racing, lowered when the requirement ends.

**OVER EARLY PROCEDURE**: If a boat is over the line at the start, the race committee will raise the X flag and sound one horn. The race committee will attempt to notify the boat by flag, radio, and voice from the committee boat. However, each sailor is responsible for recognizing if they are over early and, if so, returning completely to the pre-start side of the line — either by recrossing or rounding an end — before starting.

#### Penalties and Time Limits

**PENALTIES:**

- If a boat breaks a rule when meeting another boat, she must complete a One-Turn Penalty (one tack and one jibe).

- If a boat touches a mark, she must complete a One-Turn Penalty.

- Penalties should be taken as soon as possible after the incident, in clear water and away from other boats, so racing is not disrupted.

- If you’re unsure whether you broke a rule, it’s usually better to take the penalty right away and keep racing rather than risk disqualification.

**TIME LIMITS:**

- A boat must round the first mark within 20 minutes of her start. Boats failing to do so will be scored DNF (Did Not Finish).

- The time limit for each race is 60 minutes. If no boat finishes within 60 minutes, the race will be abandoned.

- After the first boat in your fleet finishes, all remaining boats have 15 minutes to finish. Boats still racing after that time will be scored TLE (Time Limit Expired), but will receive a score one place higher than the last boat that finished.

#### Scoring and Race Results

**SCORING**: Scoring will use the Low Point System from Appendix A of the Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS). In this system, first place earns 1 point, second place earns 2 points, third place earns 3 points, and so on. At the end of the regatta, the boat with the lowest total score wins. The Governor’s Cup will have a target of nine races.

**RACE DISCARDS**: In longer regattas, sailors may discard their worst score. If fewer than seven races are completed, all races count. If 7 to 13 races are completed, each sailor may discard one score. If 14 or more races are completed, each sailor may discard two scores.

**FAILING TO START OR FINISH A RACE**: Boats that do not start (DNS), do not finish (DNF), or are disqualified (DSQ) will score points equal to the number of boats that started the race plus one.

**BREAKING TIES**: If two or more boats are tied on points at the end of the regatta, the tie is broken by comparing their race finishes one by one. For example, if Boat A has a first place and Boat B’s best finish is second, Boat A wins the tie. If their best finishes are the same, the tie is decided by the next best finish, and so on. If they are still tied after that, the boat that finished ahead in the last race sailed will win the tie.

***Example scoring and tie-break****:* In a nine-race regatta, each sailor may discard one score.

- **Boat A finishes**: 1, 2, 2, 3, 3, 3, 4, 4, (6) → discard the 6 → total = 22 points.

- **Boat B finishes**: 2, 1, 3, 2, 4, 4, 3, 3, (5) → discard the 5 → total = 22 points.

Both boats are tied on 22 points. The tie is broken by comparing best finishes: Boat A has a first place, Boat B also has a first place. Compare next best finishes: Boat A has three second places, Boat B has two. Therefore, Boat A wins the tie.

**RACE RESULTS**: Preliminary results will be posted in the `#racing` channel on the ASC Discord server. These are provisional and may change pending protests or scoring corrections. Results discussions are a great chance to learn how scoring works, so feel free to ask questions!
Final results will be posted on the ASC website.

#### Protests and Conduct

**PROTESTS**: On-the-water resolution is encouraged whenever possible. If an incident cannot be resolved on the water, the race committee will meet after the day’s racing to hear the protest and make a ruling. Other experienced sailors may be invited to participate in the discussion, but the race committee will make the final decision. Protest discussions are intended to be a friendly learning opportunity for all sailors.

**RACE CONDUCT**: Keep it friendly! Help newcomers, encourage sportsmanship, and remember we’re here to have fun and grow our sailing community.

#### Safety and Operations

**SAFETY:** Sailors in club boats must wear personal flotation devices (PFDs) at all times. Sailors in private boats must carry PFDs on board at all times. The race committee may require all sailors to wear PFDs by displaying the Y flag on the RC flagpole. For early-season or cold-weather events, sailors should wear wetsuits or drysuits. Each sailor is responsible for deciding whether conditions are safe for them to sail. The race committee may abandon races at any time if it deems conditions unsafe.

**COMMUNICATIONS**: The race committee will monitor channel 68 and may provide announcements.

**CLUB BOAT CARE AND STORAGE**: We try to keep all club boats race-ready

- While club boats are docked, sails should be lowered. For club Lasers, the outhaul should be fully uncleated so the sail flies free of the boom. This prevents wind gusts from damaging the boats.

- Boats should be properly secured while at the dock, including a bow line, a stern line, and a spring line. The trailing end of the stern line may be used as a spring. On club Lidos, the spring can run through the jib cleat. For Buccaneers, Skipjacks, and the Catalina, tie the end of the line to the shroud with a bowline. Attach a fender where the boat might touch the dock. If you are unsure, ask an experienced sailor for help.

- Club Lasers may be left on the green small-boat launch dock during the day, but must be removed overnight. For overnight storage, leave them on the grassy area by the small boat rack, with the sail wrapped around the mast and the mast unstepped and laid on the boat.

- The club Buccaneer 18 (“Waldo”) must be removed from the water, drained, and stored on its trailer overnight.

- All sailors are responsible for putting away any boat they use for the regatta, even if they do not stay for both days of racing.

**WHAT TO BRING:**

- *Watch with a countdown timer:* Essential for timing race starts accurately.

- *Drinking water:* Staying hydrated is important, even on cooler days.

- *Snacks:* Quick energy helps you stay sharp during multiple races.

- *A warm, windproof layer:* Weather on Big Lake can change quickly, and being prepared keeps sailing safe and enjoyable.

- *Sunscreen:* Sunlight reflects strongly off the water, so protection helps prevent sunburn even on cloudy days.

A handheld VHF radio is required to race in the Buccaneer 18 fleet. Although it’s not required to race in Portsmouth, we still recommend bringing a radio because it helps to hear race committee announcements and safety information.
