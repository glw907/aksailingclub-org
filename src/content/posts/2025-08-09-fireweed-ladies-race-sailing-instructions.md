This event is designed to be fun and welcoming for sailors of all experience levels. Ladies only at the helm!

### August 9-10, 2025
Alaska Sailing Club, Big Lake, Alaska

### Event Information

**WELCOME!** The Fireweed Ladies Race is designed to be fun and welcoming for sailors of all experience levels. Don't have a boat or crew? Come anyway — we'll help you get on the water!

**RULES:** Racing will be governed by [The Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS)](https://www.sailing.org/inside-world-sailing/rules-regulations/racingrules/) and these Sailing Instructions, with an emphasis on friendly competition and encouraging newcomers.

**REGISTRATION:** No advance registration required. Just show up!

**ENTRY FEE:** None required.

### Eligibility and Boats

**ELIGIBILITY:** Women skippers only. Anyone may crew, though male crew are expected to be well-behaved and supportive of the event's welcoming atmosphere.

**BOATS:** Club boats are available for those without boats.

### Schedule

**Saturday, August 9th:**

- Skipper's meeting: 1300 (1:00 PM)

- First warning signal: 1400 (2:00 PM), wind dependent

- 3-5 races planned for afternoon/evening

**Sunday, August 10th:**

- If insufficient races are completed on Saturday, additional racing may be held.

- Group sail to brewery: Approximately 1100 (11:00 AM) start if Saturday racing is complete

### Racing Information

**RACING AREA:** Big Lake in the vicinity of the Alaska Sailing Club.

**MARKS:** Look for the orange, yellow, or red inflatable marks — these mark the course you'll sail around.

**STARTING AND FINISHING:** The start/finish line runs between a red mark and the race committee boat (the boat with the big antenna displaying signals). You'll start and finish by crossing this same line.

**COURSES:** Windward/leeward (upwind to a mark, then downwind to another mark) or windward/leeward/windward (upwind, downwind, then upwind again to finish). The course will be posted on the race committee boat.

### Race Management

**TIME LIMITS:**

- Races will be abandoned if no boat reaches the first mark within 20 minutes of the start

- Races will be abandoned if no boat finishes within 60 minutes of the start

- Boats finishing more than 30 minutes after the first boat will receive a Time Limit Expired (TLE) score

**SCORING:** Low point system. No throw-outs. 3-5 races planned.

**STARTS:** Single start for all boats. Remember to bring a watch with a timer function so you can time your starts.

**FLEETS:** All boats will start together but may be scored in separate fleets. The primary fleet will be [Portsmouth handicap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Yardstick) (different boat types can compete fairly together). One-design fleets may be formed for scoring if three or more boats of the same class participate.

**RACE CONDUCT:** Keep it friendly! Help newcomers, encourage good sportsmanship, and remember we're here to have fun and grow our sailing community.

**PROTESTS:** We encourage on-the-water resolution of minor incidents when possible. We'll gather after racing to discuss and resolve any incidents as friendly learning opportunities to help everyone grow their racing skills and knowledge of the rules.

### Safety and Communications

**SAFETY:** Life jackets are required for all participants per ASC rules.

**COMMUNICATIONS:** A handheld marine VHF radio is preferred but not required. The race committee will monitor channel 68 and may provide helpful announcements.

**RACE RESULTS:** Preliminary daily results will be posted in the `#racing` channel on the [ASC Discord server](/members/discord-server/). Final results will be posted on the [ASC website](/).

### Social Events

**POTLUCK:** Time and location are weather dependent (afternoon or evening). Any contribution is welcome!

**BREWERY SAIL:** Sunday group sail to brewery starts approximately 1100 (11:00 AM) if Saturday racing is complete.
