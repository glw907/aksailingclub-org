But wait, what the heck is a NOR? If you’re new to sailboat racing, you may not have seen a “Notice of Race” (NOR) before.

An NOR is simply the official announcement for a regatta. It lets sailors know that an event is happening and provides the key details: when and where the races will be held, what kinds of boats can enter, how to sign up, and what to expect when you arrive.

For first-time racers, the NOR is the place to learn the basics of how the regatta will run. It answers questions like: What time should I be there? What time is the skippers' meeting? How many races are planned? Do I need to bring anything special? Reading the NOR ahead of time is the best way to feel prepared and confident on race day.

The NOR is also the first step toward the more detailed *Sailing Instructions* (SIs). While the NOR gives you the big picture, the SIs explain the finer points of how the races will be conducted — things like course layouts, start sequences, and scoring. We’ll publish the SIs at least three days before the regatta so that everyone has time to review them.

And without further ado, here’s the NOR for the Northern Lights Regatta:

### Northern Lights Regatta Notice of Race (NOR)

**September 20, 2025**
**Alaska Sailing Club, Big Lake, Alaska, USA**

#### Event Overview

**WELCOME:**
The Northern Lights Regatta is our final racing event of the season. It’s a one-day-only series held on the Saturday before the fall work party and is decidedly low-key. We started this race as a homage to the highly informal “old-school” racing of the ASC’s past. The race committee usually sets a single mark to serve as one end of the starting line and uses the end of the “B” dock as the other end. Instead of using marks, we set a course using various islands on Big Lake.

This event has gained popularity in recent years due to the reliable September winds and the exciting challenge of using the landforms on the lake to the best possible advantage. Also, the shorter days improve the odds of viewing the Northern Lights while hanging by the campfire after the races!

This event is designed to be fun, welcoming, and friendly to sailors of all experience levels. If you don't have a boat or crew, come anyway — we will help you get on the water! This regatta is run in a supportive environment, with experienced sailors available to assist with rigging, answer questions, and help newer sailors learn.

Even if you would rather not race, you are welcome to sail recreationally, enjoy the lake, and join us for the potluck or other social events.

**RULES:** Racing will be governed by The Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS), except as modified by the Sailing Instructions, which will be published on the ASC website no later than three days before the Northern Lights Regatta. Any final changes to the SIs will be posted no later than two hours before the start of the first race on the day they take effect.

**ADDITIONAL INFORMATION**: We’re starting the races a bit later than usual to accommodate some of our regular races earlier in the day. But we *may* start a bit earlier depending on the timing of the XC races. If that happens, we’ll provide an updated time in the SIs. Also, we'd love to see a few more keelboats racing so TL has some competition!

#### Participation

**ELIGIBILITY:** Any member of the Alaska Sailing Club or their guest may participate. Each boat must have at least one ASC member on board. Club boats are available on a first-rigged/first-served basis, and once a skipper claims a club boat, it is theirs for the duration of the regatta.

**ENTRY:** There is no entry fee for this event. Registration is not required in advance — just come and join us on race day.

**FLEETS:**

- **Portsmouth fleet:** This fleet is open to all types of sailing dinghies. Because different boats go different speeds, we use the [Portsmouth Yardstick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Yardstick) to adjust finish times and keep the racing fair. This fleet is relaxed, beginner-friendly, and a great place for new sailors to start.

- **Buccaneer 18 fleet:** This is a one-design fleet, which means all sailors use the same type of boat: the Buccaneer 18. Because the boats are identical, the finish order is more directly based on sailing skill rather than differences in boat speed. The "Bucc fleet" is more competitive than Portsmouth, but it remains welcoming to newcomers who want to learn.

- **Keelboat fleet:** This fleet is open to small keelboats. Races will be scored using the Portsmouth Yardstick. At least two keelboats must be entered for this fleet to race.

***Additional fleet rules****:*

- If three or more boats of the same type participate, they may form their own one-design fleet at the discretion of the Principal Race Officer.

- If there are fewer than two Buccaneer 18s or two Portsmouth dinghies registered, those boats will be combined into a single Portsmouth Dinghy fleet and scored together.

**LOOKING FOR CREW OR A BOAT?** Join the [ASC Discord server](/members/discord-server/) and check the `#racing` channel.

#### Scoring & Schedule

**SCORING**: Scoring will use the Low Point System from Appendix A of the Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS). In this system, first place earns 1 point, second place earns 2 points, third place earns 3 points, and so on. At the end of the regatta, the boat with the lowest total score wins.

**SCHEDULE:**
**September 20, 2025** – The skipper’s meeting will be held at 16:00 AKDT. All boats must be fully rigged and ready to sail before the skipper’s meeting begins. Check-in happens at the skipper’s meeting. Experienced members are available to assist new sailors with rigging. But if you’d like help, please start working on your boat at least 90 minutes before the skipper’s meeting.

The first warning signal will be no earlier than 30 minutes after the skipper’s meeting, which allows time to launch and sail to the starting area. The race committee may delay the start if wind conditions are unsuitable for racing.

All races will be held on this day. The Northern Lights Regatta will have a target of 3 races, with no cutoff time for starting races. No more than five races will be sailed in a single day, and the actual number will depend on wind and weather conditions.

**SOCIAL EVENTS:** A potluck will follow the races. The theme is **warm food**, to cut the September chill.

**SPECIAL EVENTS:** On Sunday, September 21, the ASC will host the **Commodore’s Breakfast at 09:00**, followed by the **annual fall work party**. More details will be shared in a separate announcement.

***Evening sailing and potluck timing****:* Winds on Big Lake are often light and variable in the afternoon, with stronger and more consistent “evening fill” winds usually arriving after 5:00 p.m. Because of this, race starts may be delayed, and sailors should be prepared to sail later in the day. Potlucks will be scheduled around the wind conditions and may take place in the late afternoon before racing or in the evening after racing.

#### Safety and Logistics

**SAFETY:** Race participants in club boats must wear personal flotation devices (PFDs) at all times. Participants in private boats must carry PFDs on board at all times. For early-season or cold-weather events, wearing a wetsuit or drysuit is recommended.

**SAILING INSTRUCTIONS (SIs):** Full Sailing Instructions will be posted on the ASC website *at least three days before the event*. Paper copies will not be distributed at the race.
