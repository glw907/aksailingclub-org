The ASC is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with a mission to promote sailing education, water safety, and the practice of the sport for Alaskans. We're member-run: you elect the board, and the board manages the club. Governance works in layers: corporate documents establish the club's legal foundation, external agreements govern the property we manage and operate on, and member-facing rules cover what you agree to as a member. Each document below includes a plain-English summary alongside the formal text.

## Corporate Foundation

The **[Articles of Incorporation](/articles-of-incorporation/)** establish the ASC as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit under Alaska law.

The **[IRS Determination Letter](/determination-letter/)** is the formal IRS confirmation of that status, issued in 2009.

The **[Bylaws](/bylaws/)** are the primary operating document. When a question isn't covered by board policy or established practice, the bylaws are the authority.

## External Agreements

The **[ASCCA Bylaws](/ascca-bylaws/)** govern the property where the club operates. The Alaska Sailing Club Condominiums Owners Association is a separate legal entity. The ASC holds Unit H within that structure, so some property decisions require ASCCA involvement.

The **[Mat-Su Borough Land Management Agreement](/mat-su-borough-land-management-agreement/)** is a 25-year permit (2022-2047) covering the tenting area, Trailer Row, and trailered boat parking on adjacent borough land.

## Member-Facing Rules

These documents cover what members agree to directly:

- **[Member Expectations](/member-expectations/)**: site and facility rules all members agree to at signup
- **[Club Boat Use and Qualification](/club-boat-use-and-qualification/)**: policies and qualification requirements for using club boats
- **[Seasonal Storage](/seasonal-storage/)**: eligibility and rules for moorings, long-term RV parking, trailered boat parking, and rack storage

## Board and Committees

The Board of Directors manages the affairs of the corporation. Members elect directors at the annual meeting each fall. Committees handle day-to-day operations: programs and racing, membership and events, site, harbor, fleet, finances, and board development. Committees also manage the annual budget process and the director election each fall.
